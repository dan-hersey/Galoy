Bitcoin Banking, Without Replacing Your Core

The Sidecar Model allows banks to add Bitcoin capabilities as a parallel system, without forcing digital assets into legacy cores.

Most banks don't want to rip and replace their core banking system to support Bitcoin. They don't need to.

Galoy is designed to operate as a Bitcoin-native sidecar, integrating cleanly alongside an existing core banking stack to enable Bitcoin services—starting with custody and Bitcoin-backed lending—without disrupting legacy systems, payment rails, or compliance workflows.

What Is the Sidecar Model?

The sidecar model allows banks to add Bitcoin capabilities as a parallel system, rather than forcing digital assets into legacy cores that were never designed to support them.

In this architecture:

  • check_circle The existing core continues to handle fiat accounts, payments, compliance, and reporting
  • check_circle Galoy operates as a dedicated Bitcoin ledger and transaction engine
  • check_circle Systems integrate via APIs and payment hubs already in place

This lets banks add Bitcoin capabilities—without compromising control, compliance, or safety.

Where Galoy Fits in the Stack

Galoy integrates alongside the core at three critical layers: ledger, asset management, and settlement.
